※ Chris Droom Design + Direction
— — — I am a digital/graphic designer with a strong focus on typography.

I am a continuous learner. I have a rigorous, reflective practice, grounded in design theory. I seek to create welcoming environments in which people are able to confidently express themselves. I am a good listener, and I am often able to get to the core of what’s trying to be expressed and the underlying problem(s).

Reflective, rigorous practice, grounded in principles and the wider design canon

My years spent in advertising has left me very skilled at implementing and developing brands, and I am extremely confident with visual applications.

I spent a lot of time growing up in Children’s Wards with little to do apart from drawing. I didn’t realise it at the time, but I was preoccupied with letterforms and markings, in particular, brand identities. I am working to become a member of The International Society of Typographic Designers (ISTD) by the time I am forty forty-one.

2020 Good Praxis Islington Council Digital Upskilling Programme

A response to job losses and precarity due to the pandemic. We wanted to create something completely different to usual council comms, yet still fulfil its function as public service design. This was also a good chance to push our company typeface (Calibre, great capitals) a bit further. We had lots of participants say they got in touch because social media posts caught their eye. Our design team also co-created and delivered the course in partnership with Outlandish. We felt we could get away with green on white legibility as they were not crucial to understanding what this poster is trying to tell you.

Findings: The name Tech-ish was not important to those joining the course. PSD: Concepts are plainly stated. Opportunity for visual wit is lower in importance when creating this type of work.
