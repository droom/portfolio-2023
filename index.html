I spent a lot of time growing up in Children’s Wards with little to do. My main entertainment was drawing. I didn’t realise it at the time, but I was preoccupied with letterforms and markings, the most accessible ones to a child being brand identities that you just see around. I was fortunate to be able to study graphic design. This came from unwavering support from my siblings and mum. The encouragement and protective environment I was afforded is unusual for our class and immigrant background. I had also been the recipient of a lot of care and nurture which not everyone has, giving me the opportunity to pursue a career in visual arts, something that not many second-generation poor immigrants have. I was fortunate to move into the industry, something that many don’t get the opportunity to do through no fault of their own. Being in the commercial industry for so long means I have lots of experience creating things that are made to sell something. I have also learnt a lot from other designers who have been really patient and generous with their time. Over the years, I’ve thought lots about access to graphic design as a profession for people from working class or migrant backgrounds. I would love to play a role in nurturing talent. I’ve had many obstacles in life and I know what it’s like to overcome them, to be in an industry that is sometimes arrogant and exclusionary, and to come through via an unconventional route. If I can make a living from this, you can too. My way into the industry was designing Flash adverts. Principles from animation and principles from video game design. This was just tweens and later on I learned ActionScript

1996: The library at my school had some multimedia PCs. On a few of them was Corel Draw 4. So I’d spend a lot of time just noodling, staying back after school and using it until they locked up.

1997: I remember seeing this site in 1997 and it made such a big impression on me. From the very beginning, I came to ‘web design’ via a visual communication interest. Most people that were making websites back then were coming at it from a technological point of view.
